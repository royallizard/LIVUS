<li><b>Port Aggregation</b>&nbsp;combines all traffic by port number, regardless of source or destination address. For example, web traffic to both 192.168.1.1 and 192.168.1.2 would be combined into a single 'host'.</li>
<li><b>Host Aggregation</b>&nbsp;combines all traffic to a host, regardless of source or destination port number.  For example, both web and ftp traffic to 192.168.1.1 would be combined into a single 'port'.</li>
<li><b>Protocol Aggregation</b>&nbsp;combines all traffic by TCP/IP protocol (TCP, UDP or ICMP), regardless of source or destination address or port. For example, all ICMP traffic would be combined, regardless of origin or destination.</li>
<li><b>AS Aggregation</b>&nbsp; combines all NetFlow data by AS (Autonomous System) number, that is as if the source and destination address and port were all zero. For more information on AS Numbers, see <a href="http://www.faqs.org/rfcs/rfc1930.html" title="link to rfc 1930">RFC 1930</a> and the high level assignments at <a href="http://www.iana.org/assignments/as-numbers" title="link to iana assignments">IANA</a></li>
</ul>
